Description

Bread is one of the oldest and most widely consumed foods in the world, made primarily from flour and water, often with yeast or another leavening agent. When baked, the dough transforms into a soft, airy interior with a golden, sometimes crisp crust. The fermentation process, especially in yeast-leavened breads, gives bread its characteristic rise and subtle flavor, while different grains—such as wheat, rye, or barley—create variations in texture, taste, and nutritional value.

Across cultures, bread takes many forms, from crusty baguettes and hearty whole-grain loaves to flatbreads and enriched varieties made with milk, eggs, or butter. It serves as a staple food in many diets, providing carbohydrates for energy along with fiber, vitamins, and minerals, depending on the ingredients used. Simple yet versatile, bread can be enjoyed on its own, paired with spreads, or used as the foundation for countless dishes.

Ingredients

  • 3 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 tablespoon sugar
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 2–3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2¼ teaspoons (1 packet) active dry yeast
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil or melted butter

Steps

  1. Activate the yeast: In a bowl, mix warm water and sugar. Sprinkle yeast on top and let it sit for 5–10 minutes until foamy.
  2. Mix the dough: Add oil, salt, and 2 cups of flour. Mix well. Gradually add the remaining flour until a soft dough forms.
  3. Knead: Knead on a floured surface for about 8–10 minutes until smooth and elastic.
  4. First rise: Place dough in a lightly oiled bowl, cover, and let rise in a warm place for 1 hour, or until doubled in size.
  5. Shape and second rise: Punch down the dough, shape into a loaf, and place in a greased loaf pan. Cover and let rise for 30 minutes.
  6. Bake: Bake at 375°F (190°C) for 25–30 minutes, until golden brown and sounds hollow when tapped.
  7. Cool: Let cool before slicing.
