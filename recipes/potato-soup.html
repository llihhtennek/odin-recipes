Potato Soup

Home

Description

Potato soup is a warm, comforting dish made primarily from tender potatoes simmered in a flavorful broth or creamy base. As the potatoes cook, they soften and naturally thicken the soup, creating a smooth, velvety texture. Many versions include onions, garlic, and herbs for depth of flavor, while milk or cream adds richness and a satisfying, hearty consistency.

Often finished with toppings like shredded cheese, crispy bacon, green onions, or a dollop of sour cream, potato soup can be customized to suit different tastes. It’s a simple yet filling meal, especially popular during colder months, offering both nourishment and a cozy, home-cooked feel in every spoonful.

Ingredients

  • 4 medium potatoes, peeled and diced
  • 1 small onion, chopped
  • 2 cups chicken or vegetable broth
  • 1 cup milk
  • 2 tablespoons butter
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Steps

  1. Cook the potatoes: In a pot, combine diced potatoes, onion, and broth. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat and simmer for 15–20 minutes, until potatoes are tender.
  2. Add creaminess: Lightly mash some of the potatoes in the pot for a thicker texture (or blend part of the soup if you prefer it smoother).
  3. Finish the soup: Stir in butter and milk. Simmer for another 5 minutes. Add salt and pepper to taste.
  4. Serve: Enjoy as is, or top with cheese, bacon bits, or green onions for extra flavor.
